1. Introduction

The greatest barrier to sustainable lunar settlement is the tyranny of the Tsiolkovsky Rocket Equation. Transporting the immense amount of fuel required to decelerate (land) a spacecraft on the Moon drives costs to unsustainable levels. This project proposes a paradigm shift: eliminating the landing propellant entirely by utilizing basic Newtonian mechanics and Earth-based infrastructure.

2. Problem Statement

Current Method: Rockets must carry propellant accounting for 80-90% of their total mass, primarily to perform the "Landing Burn" against lunar gravity.

Current Cost: Approximately $1.2 Million USD (~40 Million THB) per kilogram of payload delivered to the lunar surface.

3. Proposed Solution

3.1 Launch: The Mass Driver

Instead of chemical rockets, we utilize an Electromagnetic Mass Driver (Linear Motor) stationed in Earth Orbit (LEO) or on the Moon's surface (for return trips).

Function: Accelerates the payload to escape velocity using solar-electric power.

Benefit: Eliminates the need for heavy first-stage boosters and launch fuel.

3.2 Landing: The "Net Sea"

A passive lithobraking system consisting of a High-Tensile Mesh stretched across a lunar crater.

Concept: A giant "catcher's mitt" for incoming payloads.

Target Material: Kevlar 49, Zylon, or Synthetic Spider Silk.

Mechanism: The payload enters the net at a calculated velocity. The net stretches deep into the crater, converting the payload's kinetic energy into elastic potential energy, gently bringing it to a stop without a single drop of fuel.

4. Feasibility Analysis

4.1 Launch Windows (Super Perigee)

We will optimize launch windows to coincide with Super Perigee events (when the Moon is closest to Earth at ~356,000 km). This minimizes travel distance and required Delta-V.

4.2 Economic Model

Preliminary calculations suggest this system reduces costs to purely electrical and maintenance expenses.

Target Cost: $100,000 USD (~3.4 Million THB) per flight.

ROI: Break-even point estimated after the 20th successful flight.

5. Conclusion

The "Shoot & Catch" system is not science fiction; it is an engineering challenge. If successful, it unlocks the "Lunar Homestead" era, making lunar habitation economically viable and sustainable for all of humanity.
